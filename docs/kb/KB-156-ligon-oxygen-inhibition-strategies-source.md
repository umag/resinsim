# Source: Ligon — Strategies to Reduce Oxygen Inhibition in Photoinduced Polymerization

**Ligon, Husár, Wutzel, Holman, Liska, "Strategies to Reduce Oxygen Inhibition
in Photoinduced Polymerization," *Chemical Reviews* 2014, 114, 557–589,
doi:10.1021/cr3005197.** (Paywalled; headline numbers corroborated by Dendukuri
2008 and Hoyle 2004.)

## What it is

The canonical review of oxygen inhibition in photopolymerization — mechanism,
rate constants, and mitigation strategies. The single most-cited overview.

## Key data

- Rate constants: R•+O₂ ~5×10⁸–10⁹ M⁻¹s⁻¹ (near diffusion-limited); propagation
  k_p ~10²–10⁴ M⁻¹s⁻¹; dissolved [O₂] ~10⁻³ M.
- Two inhibition pathways: excited-photoinitiator quenching + radical scavenging
  to unreactive peroxyl radicals (dominant).
- Mitigation strategies: high initiator load, amine/thiol H-donors, inert
  atmosphere, oxygen-consuming additives.
