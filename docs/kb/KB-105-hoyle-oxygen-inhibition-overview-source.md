# Source: Hoyle — An Overview of Oxygen Inhibition in Photocuring

**Hoyle, "An Overview of Oxygen Inhibition in Photocuring," RadTech e|5 2004.**

## What it is

A canonical, citable overview of the oxygen-inhibition mechanism in free-radical
photocuring — the clean one-liner source for dissolved-O₂ concentration and the
peroxyl-radical scheme.

## Key data

- Verbatim: *"oxygen…is present at a concentration of 10⁻² to 10⁻³ M in most
  photocurable resins [and] inhibits free-radical polymerization."*
- Canonical inhibition scheme: propagating radical `P• + O₂ → PO₂•`
  (chain-terminating peroxyl radical).
- Establishes O₂ as the dominant induction-period species before cure.
