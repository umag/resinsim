# Source: The effect of monomer structure on oxygen inhibition of (meth)acrylates

**"The effect of monomer structure on oxygen inhibition of (meth)acrylates
photopolymerization," *Polymer* 2004.** (Publisher paywall; mined via search +
corroborated by Hoyle 2004.)

## What it is

Structure–property study of how monomer chemistry controls O₂ inhibition and the
polymerization-onset threshold.

## Key data

- Equilibrium dissolved [O₂] ≈ 10⁻³ mol/L in typical (meth)acrylate monomer.
- Polymerization begins only after [O₂] falls to **a few tenths of a percent**
  of its air-saturated value.
- Higher-viscosity / multifunctional monomers slow O₂ diffusion; alkylene-oxide
  and thioether groups chemically consume O₂ — both reduce inhibition.
- Acrylates are markedly more O₂-sensitive than methacrylates.
