# Source: Mrázek — Continuous printing on an LCD resin printer (O₂ reservoir)

**Honza Mrázek, "Continuous printing on LCD resin printer — no more wasted time
on peeling?" (blog, 2022).**

## What it is

A hobbyist experiment probing an O₂-permeable silicone window on an LCD printer —
the (anecdotal, cautionary) source for the O₂-reservoir depletion claim.

## Key data

- Verbatim: *"That oxygen is…consumed/taken away after 10 peel cycles… when you
  leave it for 30 minutes on the air, it can again print."* → ~10-layer reservoir,
  ~30 min recovery.
- **Important caveat: the experiment FAILED** — the silicone film *increased*
  peel force ~5× vs FEP and did not achieve continuous printing. Cite as
  depletion-kinetics folklore, NOT proof that passive O₂ reservoirs work.
