# Source: Scherzer — Oxygen solubility in acrylates

**Scherzer et al., *Macromolecular Chemistry and Physics* 2005,
doi:10.1002/macp.200400300.**

## What it is

Measured dissolved-O₂ solubility across a range of acrylate monomers, and its
temperature dependence — the source for the acrylate-specific (not water)
solubility figure.

## Key data

- O₂ solubility is monomer-structure-dependent: **0.59×10⁻³ mol/L** (ethoxylated
  HEMA) to **2.07×10⁻³ mol/L** (butyl acrylate).
- ~4× higher than air-saturated water (⇒ the "~8 ppm" water figure is wrong for
  resin; acrylate is ~30 ppm by mass).
- Temperature dependence of O₂ solubility is the primary driver of the
  induction-period shortening on heating.
